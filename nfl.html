2025 NFL Season · Final Recap
2025 SEASON IN REVIEW
The 2025 NFL season is complete. Denver and Seattle dominated their conferences. Here's how it all went down.
2025 Season Storylines
Denver Broncos Dominate the AFC
AFC Conference Champions · 14-3 record
▾

The Denver Broncos were the class of the AFC in 2025, finishing with the best record in the conference at 14-3. Their quarterback situation, which was a question mark entering the season, stabilized quickly and their defense was elite all year long. The Mile High altitude remained a significant home field advantage. They cruised through the AFC playoffs and represented the conference in the Super Bowl.

Seattle Seahawks' NFC Dominance
NFC Conference Champions · 14-3 record
▾

Seattle matched Denver with a 14-3 record, the best in the NFC. The CenturyLink crowd was as loud as ever and the Seahawks' combination of a powerful running game and accurate quarterback play made them nearly impossible to beat. They handled business in the NFC playoffs and set up a compelling Super Bowl matchup between two 14-3 teams.

Kansas City Chiefs Fall from Grace
AFC West · 6-11 record
▾

After years of dominance, the Kansas City Chiefs had a stunning collapse in 2025, finishing 6-11 and missing the playoffs. Patrick Mahomes dealt with injuries and the offensive line struggled all season. It was the kind of down year that reminded everyone that dynasties don't last forever. The Chiefs will be highly motivated heading into 2026 and are expected to be a major free agency player this offseason.

New England Patriots' Resurgence
AFC East Champions · 14-3 record
▾

After several rough post-Brady years, the New England Patriots roared back to relevance with a 14-3 season under their new head coach. The Patriots drafted well, developed their quarterback, and returned to their ball-control, defensive identity. AFC East rivals Buffalo and Miami were competitive but couldn't match New England's consistency week in and week out.

Jacksonville Jaguars Break Through
AFC South Champions · 13-4 record
▾

Jacksonville continued their rise with a 13-4 season and another AFC South title. Trevor Lawrence has developed into a legitimate top-5 quarterback and the Jaguars' fanbase has fully bought in. The city of Jacksonville has embraced this team and the playoff atmosphere at EverBank Stadium was electric all season. They were a legit Super Bowl contender before falling in the divisional round.
