HORNETS 118·CELTICS 89
Charlotte, NC · March 4
UPSET OF WEEK▾

Nobody saw this coming. The Charlotte Hornets walked into TD Garden on Wednesday night and handed the Boston Celtics their most lopsided home loss of the season — a 118-89 demolition that was never competitive after the first quarter.

Knueppel: 29 PTS, 3 STL
CHA 2nd Half: 65 PTS
Brown (BOS): 21 PTS

Rookie Kon Knueppel poured in 29 points on 11-of-17 shooting. Charlotte outscored Boston 65-42 in the second half, turning a competitive game into a statement. This is their sixth straight win. They are 32-31, above .500 for the first time since December, and a genuine Play-In threat.

THUNDER 103·KNICKS 100
New York, NY · March 4
CLUTCH FINISH▾

SGA returned from his abdominal strain and Oklahoma City needed every bit of him. The Knicks pushed the league's best team to the wire inside Madison Square Garden, trading blows in a fourth quarter that had both benches on their feet.

Holmgren: 28 PTS, 11 REB
SGA: 26 PTS
KAT: 17 PTS, 17 REB

With 42 seconds left and the game tied at 100, Jrue Holiday stepped into a corner three and buried it. Thunder 103, Knicks 100. OKC is now 49-15 — the franchise has never won a title. This team intends to change that.

HAWKS 131·BUCKS 113
Atlanta, GA · March 4
W5 COMEBACK▾

Atlanta trailed by 16 at halftime and outscored Milwaukee 65-42 in the second half. Nickeil Alexander-Walker led with 23 off the bench, Okongwu added 21 on 9-of-12. The Hawks are 32-31 — first winning record since December. Milwaukee falls to 26-35.

CLIPPERS 130·PACERS 107
Los Angeles, CA · March 4
HISTORIC NIGHT▾
